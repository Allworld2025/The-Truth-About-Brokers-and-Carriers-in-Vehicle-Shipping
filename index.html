Understanding the Key Players

When shipping a vehicle, you’ll encounter two main entities: brokers and carriers. Understanding their roles—and the myths surrounding them—is essential to making an informed decision. In this post, we’ll break down the truth about how each works, how they differ, and how AmeriFreight simplifies the entire process.

Myth #1: Brokers Are Just Middlemen Who Increase the Cost

This is one of the biggest misconceptions. While brokers do act as intermediaries, they also provide immense value. A reputable broker like AmeriFreight helps you find the most reliable carrier at a competitive price, ensures paperwork is in order, and offers customer support throughout your shipment.

Myth #2: Going Direct with a Carrier Is Always Better

Working directly with a carrier can sometimes offer slightly lower rates, but it often comes with trade-offs: limited availability, less scheduling flexibility, and a lack of customer service. Plus, you're responsible for vetting the carrier yourself—something a broker already handles.

What Brokers and Carriers Actually Do

Brokers

  • Match your shipment with the right carrier
  • Negotiate rates on your behalf
  • Ensure carriers are licensed and insured
  • Manage scheduling and communication
  • Offer customer support and shipment tracking

Carriers

  • Own and operate the transport trucks
  • Physically move the vehicle
  • Follow broker or customer instructions
  • Manage pickups and deliveries
  • Focus on transport logistics
